Research Methods and Professional Practice

This page outlines my progress throughout the Research Methods and Professional Practice (RMPP) module. Narratives of each unit follow below, including my weekly reflections, activities performed, and links to artefacts.

Unit 1: Scientific Investigation and Ethics

Reflection: This module began by introducing the concept of academic research and, most importantly, underscoring the importance of ethics in the field. Many reading assignments in this unit identified the distinction between ethics and morality, which has helped me understand my own position. Though ethics are critical to the respect and discipline in our field, I feel that morality has a special place in the decision-making process and ultimately determines how well I’ll sleep at night. As I read about scientific investigation and research, the equities of ethics and morality were generally aligned. However, when reading about conducting offensive cyber operations to disable predatory networks, ethics and morality certainly clashed.

    Activities Performed:
  • Selected a research topic for the Literature Review and Presentation assignments.
  • Conducted research and developed an Initial Post for Collaborative Discussion 1 (link to Collaborative Discussion 1).
  • Completed the Reasoning Quiz (link to Quiz results).
  • Completed Reflective Activity 1 – Ethics in Computing (link to Reflective Activity 1).

Unit 2: Research Questions, the Literature Review and the Research Proposal

Reflection: This week introduced literature reviews and research proposals. Though I have read hundreds of literature reviews over the years, I have never produced one – so I enjoyed taking a moment to understand the underlying methodology and degree of labour required. Similarly, I have written scores of business proposals over the years, but never a research proposal. As with other types of proposals, I learned that research proposals must clearly describe your goal, the means by which you will achieve that goal, and why your approach will work. This has reinforced my appreciation for creative approaches to problem solving – research, or otherwise – and the use of effective communication to obtain audience buy-in.

    Activities Performed:
  • Listened to the “Research Questions, the Literature Review and the Research Proposal” Lecturecast.
  • Read and responded to classmates’ posts for Collaborative Discussion 1 (link to Collaborative Discussion 1).
  • Began the Literature Review and Research Proposal Outlines e-Portfolio Activity (link to Literature Review Outline).
  • Assessed Literature Review Guide Questions.

Unit 3: Research Methods

Reflection: This was one of my favourite units throughout my MSc programme. Research design allows me to figure out how to solve a research problem, using different modalities to learn about a problem. This week’s reading assignments reinforced my notion that research methods offer different ways of collecting and synthesising information from data, with each method bringing its own strengths and weaknesses. I learned that combining research methodologies produces a multi-dimensional perspective of a problem, and ultimately delivers more meaningful and accurate results. I believe that research design is an art as much as it is a science, and benefits from a holistic understanding of the problem.

    Activities Performed:
  • Submitted a Summary Post for Collaborative Discussion 1 (link to Collaborative Discussion 1).

Unit 4: Case Studies, Focus Groups and Observations

Reflection: : During Unit 4, I learned about different observation methods to conduct primary research. For my literature review, I have decided to research the application of machine learning in music recommender systems (MRS); specifically, I want to determine if the use of MRS impacts the musical preferences of its users. I believe that many of the observation methods discussed in this week – such as focus groups and case studies – may be prone to bias through subjective interpretations and anecdotal evidence. The more I learn about music preference formation, the more I see a need to isolate subjective variables and create apples-to-apples quantitative comparisons. Furthermore, it seems that many studies in this field may inadvertently impact the results – so discreet observation will be important.

Unit 5: Interviews and Survey Methods

Reflection: For as long as I can remember, I have enjoyed figuring out how to get information from people (note – that sounds more sinister than intended!). The reading and activities during this week revisited the importance of ethics in survey design and resultant data usage, and how seemingly innocuous interactions with questionnaires may end up providing information for purposes beyond the respondent’s awareness. This is a clear violation of research ethics. In further reading, I realised that the handling of research data is just as important as ethically conducting research in the first place.

    Activities Performed:
  • Completed the “Interviews and Survey Design” Lecturecast.
  • Completed Reflective Activity 2 - Case Study: Inappropriate Use of Surveys (link to Reflective Activity 2).

Unit 6: Questionnaire Design

Reflection: During this unit, I learned about conscious and unconscious bias, and how to develop questions that improve the objectivity, measurement, and analysis of responses. Before this unit, I believed that asking open-ended questions was not particularly useful to research due to the inherent variance in response possibilities; however, it appears that qualitative analysis techniques can make use of this variance to discern valuable insights. While I gravitate toward quantitative analysis, and finding ways to constrain qualitative analysis, the use of open-ended questions is a technique I hope to explore in future research problems.

    Activities Performed:
  • Completed the “Questionnaire Design” Lecturecast.
  • Focussed on the completion of the summative Literature Review assignment.

Unit 7: Validity and Generalisability in Research

Reflection: This unit taught me how to establish credibility in research efforts through reliability, generalisability, and validity. After reflecting upon this week’s reading assignments and activities, I believe that transparency in research is critical to the scientific community, as well as the greater public. Research must be trusted in order to be useful. I also appreciate that researchers may interpret results differently, or infer different conclusions; however, the underlying experiment methodology should be sound. After reading hundreds of research papers for my literature review, I have learned to appreciate that debate in the scientific community is healthy and benefits the pursuit of knowledge.

    Activities Performed:

Unit 8: Inferential Statistics

Reflection: As a computer science graduate student, statistics are vitally important to my field of study. This week refreshed my knowledge on how to apply inferential statistics to research populations, and how to ensure that quantitative measurements are conducted in a standardised manner recognised by the scientific community. I also expanded my knowledge on the use of descriptive statistics to describe research results, which I will incorporate into my Research Presentation assignment. I grew up thinking of problems and results in terms of numbers: this unit has reintroduced concepts that will help me better communicate analytical methodologies and research findings in the future.

Unit 9: Analysing Qualitative Data

Reflection: I have always found qualitative analysis to be a difficult methodology to master due to its subjectivity. However, it often unlocks the story behind quantitative results. This week helped refine my qualitative analysis skills by introducing frameworks and approaches to standardise qualitative responses into usable data. I also learned techniques to minimise variance in the interpretation of qualitative results, mitigating some of the difficulties I have previously faced with this research methodology. This unit also gave me insight into many of the business research surveys I have participated in over the years.

    Activities Performed:

Unit 10: Research Writing

Reflection: Unit 10 has been very useful in the context of my professional career. One of my primary activities is to propose the implementation of novel technologies and technical solutions to prospective clients. I take pride in my ability to communicate; however, I have noticed that my communication style resonates more with military and operational audiences than academic circles. I believe that the writing techniques learned in this week will not only help me with my impending dissertation but will improve my ability to communicate with researchers. This week also gave me a better idea of what to expect in the final Project module for my dissertation.

Useful Resources

During this module, I used the following tools the most:
  • Adobe Premiere – one of the industry standard video editing applications. This has a free trial option, but better yet, Adobe offers the entire Creative Cloud Suite at a drastically discounted rate for students https://www.adobe-students.com/creativecloud/buy/students.html
  • GitHub – the gold standard for version control systems and developing across a team: https://github.com/
  • Lucidchart – offers a free tier for students that is full featured but limited to 3 simultaneous documents. My favourite tool for UML and diagramming, by far: https://www.lucidchart.com/pages/usecase/education
  • JetBrains PyCharm – a great IDE for Python that supports many plug-ins and extensions, including Git integration. Jetbrains offers students free use of their software: https://www.jetbrains.com/community/education/#students
  • Notepad++ - a great, simple, FREE text editor for Windows. Supports multiple tabs, sophisticated editing of any file that’s text based: https://notepad-plus-plus.org/
  • Sublime Text – my favourite text editor on Mac. Like Notepad ++ but with a sleeker interface, and slightly fewer features. Trial version available for free: https://www.sublimetext.com
