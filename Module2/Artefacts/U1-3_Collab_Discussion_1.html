Object-Oriented Information Systems, Unit 1-3

Collaborative Discussion 1: Information System Failure

Summary Post

Of interest to the current geopolitical stand-off between Russia and the West, nuclear missile detection systems for both the United States and Soviet Union experienced errors which produced false alarms during the Cold War (Stevens & Mele, 2018). A series of false alarms occurred on 3 June 1980, when a defective integrated microchip caused the number “2” to replace some zeros in the data field storing the number of missiles detected (National Security Archive, 2020).  As a result, the North American Aerospace Defense Command (NORAD) reported that 2,200 missiles were heading toward the United States (Stevens & Mele, 2018).  Worse yet, this alert occurred shortly after the Soviet Union had invaded Afghanistan, so the U.S. was already hyper-vigilant for Soviet aggression (Schlosser, 2016). The American National Security Advisor, Zbigniew Brzezinski, received a phone call just before 2:30am, who in turn initiated nuclear response protocols: preparing to evacuate the President, launching airborne command centres, and readying Minuteman nuclear-armed missiles for retaliatory strikes (Nuclear Age Peace Foundation, 2019). Thankfully, Brezezinski received a call within minutes stating that this was a false alarm – missile warning sensors did not corroborate the report, and retaliatory measures were cancelled minutes before the U.S. would have launched missiles against the Soviet Union (National Security Archive, 2020).

Expounding on this point, Wang Wang noted that data accuracy within a system should not solely rely on a single fallible data source – particularly when the implications of inaccuracy jeopardise lives (2022). This pattern of reliance upon a single data source was also observed in the Boeing 737 MAX failures (Wang, 2022).

Further, Mohammad Atieh commented that system testing should be conducted in accordance with the likelihood of a given system error, and more importantly, the subsequent consequences if such an error were to occur (2022).
